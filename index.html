About Me

I was born in a small town called Manipal, but I grew up in Kochi, a place that has shaped a lot of who I am today. I was a super energetic but a simple kid, who if she could dance would be happy.One of my most vivid childhood memories is dancing endlessly in front of the TV to Bollywood songs. Dance was my happy place back then, and honestly, it still is today. I was also the kind of kid who got curious about numbers and loved stories. That mix has stayed with me, and over time it’s become something I want to use in a bigger way. My dream is pretty simple: to take what fascinates me — numbers and storytelling — and use it to do some good in the world.

How I fell in love with Analytics

My love for analytics and data science actually came from my brother. I used to listen to him get super excited about how data is everywhere — like, literally all around us — and how we all use it in some way every day. It kind of stuck with me and made me want to explore it myself. As I started my professional journey, I quickly realized that the decisions that really make a difference in business aren’t just about intuition—they’re about having a solid understanding of the numbers behind them. I found myself fascinated by how data could tell a story, highlight opportunities, and even prevent mistakes before they happen. Seeing the impact of insights applied in real-world scenarios made me appreciate just how powerful analytics can be, and it motivated me to dive deeper into learning how to use data thoughtfully and effectively.

What lies ahead?

I’m currently pursuing my Master’s in Business Analytics at the University of Southern California, and every day here has been an opportunity to explore my love for data and problem-solving. From attending workshops to participating in events, I’ve been able to challenge myself and gain experiences that are shaping the way I approach analytics in the real world. Through this journey, I’ve discovered that my true interests lie in the healthcare, entertainment, and CPG industries, and I’m excited about the possibility of contributing as an analyst in one of them after graduation. Right now, I’m looking for a summer 2026 internship in data or business analytics — a role where I can continue learning, applying my skills, and helping solve meaningful problems. If you think I might be a fit for a role, I’d love to connect — you can reach out to me through the “Contact Me” page.
